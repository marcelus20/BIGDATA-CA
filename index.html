A Brief explanation of Bitcoin

Bitcoin is an innovative payment network and a new kind of money. Bitcoin uses peer-to-peer technology to operate with no central authority or banks; managing transactions and the issuing of bitcoins is carried out collectively by the network. Bitcoin is open-source; its design is public, nobody owns or controls Bitcoin and everyone can take part. Through many of its unique properties, Bitcoin allows exciting uses that could not be covered by any previous payment system.

Info About a specific block

A block is a collenction of transactions performed by the network. Each block stores around 2500 transactions and after it has reached its store limit, it needs to be validated. Validation means that the new hash for this block needs to be discovered and this task is performed by the miners. When the block is validated, this new hash is linked with the next block "previous hash" attribute and here is where the blockchain comes from. The number of zeros in the begining is determined by the difficulty reate. The more defficult the more zeros there will be. In order to discover the hash of the block, there is not a better approach other than brute force combinations of nonces. It works like this:

  • Let the SHA256(currentBlockState) = x.
  • Network will apply the difficulty rate, so x will become 00000000x;
  • Hashing goes one way around, therefore the miner will have to combine SHA256(nonce + currentBlockState);
  • SHA256(0 + currentBlockState) == 00000000x? No. Make nonce 1, is it equals to 00000000x? No, make nonce 2... Until the hash matches.

Expected time of Arival for the next block

The algorithm is adjusted to regulate the difficulty rate so that the everage time to mine one blocks keeps neither more nor less than 10 minutes. The more processing power provided by the miners there is, the quicker a hash of the block could be found.

For this reason, the algorithm encrease the difficulty reate if there is too many processing power else diminish rate if power is reduced to always keep to ten minutes average.

The value below means how long for the current block to be mined. If the value is negative, it means that the network is taking more than 10 minutes to mine, hence the algorithm will adjust the difficulty at some point.

Current Reward for a mined Bitcoin block

The rewarding system is the source of inflation of bitcoin. Every ten minutes, the value below is created and rewared to the miner that could validate the last block before all the other miners. A miner can be a computer, or a group of computer combining resources to increase the chances of validating block before everybody else.

Back in 2009, when bitcoin was created, the reward was of 50 BTC per block. After 210000 validated blocks, the reward dropped down by half. So, in 2013, the rewarding value went down from 50 BTC/Block to 25 BTC/Block. In 2016 it again dropped to 12.5 BTC/block and it will remain like this until next Halving.

The process of dropping down the reward every 210000 blocks (4 years) is called Halving.

Next Bitcoin Halving is going to happen in May/2020 and the reward is going down to 6.25 BTC. Because every 4 years the having happens, the inflation rate decreases in time. As the consensus protocol states that the limit is 21 millios of BTC in circulation, the last satoshi is to be mined around in the year 2140.
